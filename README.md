# Workshop | “AI as Strategic Test Assistance”

Welcome to the workshop.

This session is designed as a practical and approachable introduction to using AI within software testing — not as a replacement for testers, but as a strategic assistant that can strengthen thinking, accelerate exploration, and support decision-making.

Many testers today are curious about AI but uncertain where to begin. Some experimented early and walked away frustrated. Others feel overwhelmed by the noise, hype, and rapidly changing tools. This workshop exists to bridge that gap in a realistic, grounded, and human-centred way.

## What You Will Explore

Throughout the workshop, we focus on practical testing activities rather than abstract AI theory. You will explore how AI can support areas such as:

* Test idea generation
* Exploratory testing
* Risk identification
* Perspective expansion
* Scenario brainstorming
* Structured analysis
* Communication support

The workshop is intentionally hands-on and low pressure. No previous AI experience is required.

You will first approach testing challenges using your own experience and professional judgement. From there, we gradually introduce AI assistance so you can compare approaches, evaluate outcomes, and understand where AI genuinely helps — and where human expertise remains essential.

## Human Oversight Matters

A strong focus is placed on critical thinking and responsible usage. AI can be fast, creative, and surprisingly useful, but it can also become confidently incorrect, inconsistent, or fictional. Learning to recognise those situations is a key part of the session.

## Workshop Goals

By the end of the workshop, the goal is that you will:

* Feel more confident using AI in daily testing work
* Understand practical prompting techniques
* Recognise strengths and limitations of AI assistance
* Learn structured ways to collaborate with AI
* Know how to validate AI-generated information
* Leave with practical methods you can immediately apply in your own projects

This workshop is not about replacing testers.

It is about helping testers strengthen their capabilities with modern tools while keeping human judgement, curiosity, and critical thinking at the centre of the testing process.
